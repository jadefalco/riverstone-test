Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Planning an excavation project comes with questions. Here are answers to some of the most common things homeowners, builders, and developers ask before getting started.

Rain is a normal part of working in British Columbia. Our crew plans ahead by protecting excavation areas whenever necessary and adjusting work safely to minimize delays and protect your property.

Safety is our highest priority. For deeper excavations we use the appropriate protection methods—including trench boxes, shoring, sloping, or benching—while carefully evaluating soil conditions before work begins.

Although underground utilities are identified before excavation whenever possible, unexpected situations can occur. If a line is damaged, work stops immediately, the area is secured, and the appropriate utility provider is contacted so the issue can be handled safely.

Yes. Existing foundations often require extra care, especially if there are signs of cracking, settling, or high groundwater. We assess site conditions carefully and use the proper equipment and techniques to protect surrounding structures.

If your project is close to neighbouring properties, it's always a good idea to let them know work will be taking place. It helps avoid surprises and allows everyone to prepare for temporary noise or equipment activity.

Every project is completed with safety in mind. Before excavation begins we assess the site, identify underground utilities, evaluate soil conditions, and follow safe excavation practices throughout the project.

Our fleet includes excavators, skid steers, trenching equipment, dump trucks, and other specialized machinery. We choose the right equipment for each project to work efficiently while minimizing disruption to your property.

Every project is different.
